In the evening, enjoy a guided visit to Chandni Chowk to explore its vibrant local markets. The rest of the evening is at your leisure for independent activities. Overnight stay in Delhi.

In the morning, your sightseeing tour begins with a visit to the bustling walled city of Old Delhi, rich with history and vibrant street life. Experience the grandeur of the Red Fort, once the seat of Mughal power, and visit Jama Masjid, India’s largest mosque built in the 17th century.

Next, drive through New Delhi to see iconic landmarks such as Raj Ghat, India Gate, Rashtrapati Bhawan (Presidential Palace), the Parliament House, and other impressive colonial-era buildings designed by the renowned British architect Edwin Lutyens.

Conclude the day with visits to the majestic Qutub Minar and the stunning Humayun’s Tomb, a magnificent example of Persian-influenced Mughal architecture.

In the evening, enjoy exploring Delhi’s vibrant local markets. Overnight stay in Delhi.

After breakfast, proceed to Agra to explore the architectural marvels of the Mughal golden era. Upon arrival, visit the Agra Fort, an impressive blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture, originally built by the Rajputs and later enhanced by various Mughal emperors using red sandstone, bricks, and exquisite white marble from Makrana.

Next, visit the iconic Taj Mahal, a timeless symbol of eternal love situated on the southern bank of the Yamuna River. This stunning monument was commissioned by the fifth Mughal Emperor, Shah Jahan, in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away in the 16th century. The Taj Mahal’s breathtaking symmetry and intricate white marble inlays continue to captivate visitors worldwide.

Important Note: The Taj Mahal is closed on Fridays.

In the evening, explore the bustling local bazaars where the descendants of the artisans who crafted the Taj Mahal still practice their exquisite marble craftsmanship. Overnight stay in Agra.

After breakfast, proceed to Jaipur, the vibrant capital of Rajasthan famously known as the "Pink City." En route, visit Fatehpur Sikri, the magnificent and well-preserved deserted Mughal royal city that served as Emperor Akbar’s political capital for 14 years before being abandoned in 1585 due to a severe drought.

Conclude the day by exploring Jaipur’s lively evening markets, renowned for their colorful handicrafts and local artistry. Overnight stay in Jaipur.

This morning, you will drive just outside Jaipur to visit the majestic Amer Fort, the former capital of the Jaipur Empire, where you can enjoy an elephant ride or jeep ride (optional, on your own). On the way to Amer, you will stop to admire the iconic Hawa Mahal.

Later, visit the City Palace and take a stroll to Jantar Mantar, the 17th-century astronomical observatory.

The rest of the evening is at your leisure for shopping and personal activities. Overnight stay at the Jaipur hotel.

Today, we conclude your ‘Golden Triangle Special’ tour. A Travelxploria Representative will assist you with your transfer to Jaipur Airport or Railway Station for your onward journey.
